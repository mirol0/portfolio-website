Ultrant: A Motivational Financial App

The Vision

Ultrant is a motivational tool for personal finance goals, that is both accessible and positive. According to Forbes magazine, 2021 is going to be a game changer year for fintech. A lot of it boils down to two things: more users are switching to online tools and a priority shift with this new administration. Financial health is going to be discussed in more detail and become part of the social consciousness in a new way, which is in large part, due to new financial health scores that will lend to exciting, more comprehensible conversations for personal finance.

Enter Ultrant: the money saving tool that is for any-and-everyone. With cool, calm colors, friendly imagery and simplistic tone Ultrant emphasizes a calm, confident approach to money saving that everyone from your college-age cousin to your empty-nester couple can appreciate. This app was designed for people that might need a little motivation to save towards their goals, and it also provides motivational content to keep personal financial goals on track. Ultrant is open minded, inclusive and compassionate; committed to a more equitable world, starting with your wallet. No goal is too big or too small. So c’mon, join Ultrant and get actionable ultra-finance advice today & everyday.

    • Find the (Design) North Star

    The main objective was to help people save money by creating a mobile personal finance app that is motivational and accessible, both in terms of tech-ability and cross-platform use. Knowing personal finance can be a prickly area for many people, Ultrant utilizes a very friendly and inviting interface that makes users feel comfortable and accomplished for creating and sticking to their financial goals.

    Research and Discovery

    I conducted research on 2 major competitors in the personal finance space: Ellevest and Mint. Ellevest, seen below on the left, has a professional, yet conversational design language that infuses trust into every task. While, Mint, on the other hand, relies on a soothing, cool blue color and easy to understand onboarding information to ensure quick comprehension. I knew these were elements I wanted my app to possess as well.

    An MVP for Ultrant already existed and now it was up to me to spruce up the user experience and interface. I focused on 3 key functions listed in the MVP:

    • As a new user, I need to be able to create a profile, so that the financial advice I receive is personalized to me, my spending habits, and tailored to my goals.
    • As a user, I want the money-saving tool I use to be motivational, so that I stay on track with my goals.
    • As a user, I want to receive a personalized savings plan, so that I can save enough money to reach my goal in time.

    With these in mind, I sought to better understand the problem space. I conducted interviews (12 total) to determine other motivations and pain points users experience with their fintech apps. From there I had a much better vision for the app and target audience and created personas based on them, seen below. Building further upon the user stories and personas, I created a site map in order to visualize the information hierarchy and provide structure for the app. After that, I focused on creating wireframes for the primary flow: adding a personalized goal.

    The style guide for the project was next. Consumer Affairs reports that only 20% of consumers prefer a visit to a physical bank over a digital experience. Another statistic states that over half of Americans delay making financial decisions. What this says is there are a lot of individuals looking to have a positive experience with their personal finance tool, and they need a little guidance in making decisions to save and invest their money properly. That’s why Ultrant relies on dark blues and friendly illustrations are used to instill trust, calm, and inclusiveness. I wanted to combine the calm, professional tone of the fintech industry with the warm and friendly illustrations and casual, creative tone of a mental-health and wellness-type brand.

    Bringing it all together, I created a deliverables bundle including low- and high-fidelity wireframes, mobile mockups, a design system, user flows and a plan for potential product add-on features.

    Final Design: Mission Accomplished

    Key Design Feature: Easy Onboarding

    This is the first thing users see after creating a login. Knowing users are not yet familiar with the interface it is extremely important to be as clear and helpful as possible in guiding them through the process. This feature is fairly common among fintech apps, and one that is highly valuabe, which is why I felt it was necessary to dedicate a user flow to it.

    Key Design Feature: Adding Personalized Goals

    Another important feature is allowing users to save for what is important to them.

    Key Design Feature: Motivational Content

    What makes Ultrant extra special is the personalized motivational content that aims to keep users on task and complete their goals.

    Conclusion

    Areas of Improvement

    Being very clear on the design strategy helped the rest of this design fall into place easily later. Throughout this process I learned to be more organized with documentation and handoff and more iterative in my design process.
