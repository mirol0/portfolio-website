Ultrant

The problem: The objective of the design brief was to help people save money quickly in preparation for a big purchase or expenditure by creating a mobile personal finance app that is accessible for users on the go, and at all levels of tech know-how.

The solution: Design a motivational tool for personal finance goals that is accessible and supportive.

Role: User Research, UX/UI Design

Resources: Adobe XD, Balsamiq, Canva, Ellevest, inVision, Mint, Miro, Paper and Pen, Storyset, UXPressia

My Process

Define

For this design brief, the main objective was to help people save money quickly in preparation for a big purchase or expenditure by creating a mobile personal finance app that is accessible for users on the go, and at all levels of tech know-how. After conducting research on the fintech app competitors, I found there was space in the market for a tool that not only provides a place to input your financia information and organize it into goals, but provides a creative, confident, and supportive voice. Enter Ultrant: your personalized motivational tool for personal saving goals. This tool is designed to be used by ANY and EVERYone, so we can all achieve our msot fabulous saving goals.

I conducted research on several competitors and quickly realized there is room in the market for a tool that not only provides a place to input your financial information and organize it into goals, but also does it in a creative, confident and supportive manner.

Two of my favorites, Ellevest and Mint, both had traits that inspired me.

Ellevest has a professional, yet conversational design language that infuses trust into every task.

Mint relies on a soothing, cool, blue color and easy to understand onboarding information to ensure quick comprehension.

Research

The next phase of my research required me to investigate the ideal user. The design brief provided ample information about the target user and their motivations. The 3 I focused on for this project design were:

- As a new user, I need to be able to create a profile, so that the financial advice I receive is personalized to me, my spending habits, and tailored to my goals.

- As a user, I want the money-saving tool I use to be motivational, so that I stay on track with my goals.

- As a user, I want to receive a personalized savings plan, so that I can save enough money to reach my goal in time.

With these stories in mind, I conducted some interviews to determine other motivations and pain points users experience. Then, with the help of the tool UXPressia, I created personas as a visual inspiration, and as a reminder as to who this service is targeting.

Ideation

The word accessible was emphasized in the design brief. I decided I wanted the design to be accessible in many ways; from how it is accessed and how the information is absorbed. I wanted to combine the calm, professional tone of the fintech industry with the warm and friendly illustrations and casual, creative tone of a mental-health and wellness-type brand. Enter ultrant: the personalized motivational tool for personal money saving goals. It is a tool designed for anyone and everyone because we all deserve to achieve our most fabulous goals.

Knowing the motivational tone of the app is the functionality that sets it apart from competitors, I decided to prioritize this task for for this iteration, as part of my design strategy.

Wireframe

At this stage, I take my user personas and user flow to begin designing the screens. Initially, paper and pen are used to draw quick low-fidelity wireframes that can be adapted easily. After those feel strong, it is time to transfer them digitally. I used Balsamiq to create initial versions of mid-fidelity wireframes.

With my mid-fidelity wireframes completed, I turned to inVision to create prototypes, My 8 person sample size, made up of members of my social and professional networks, tested the prototypes and provided consistent feedback that the log in flow needed some clarification, as well as the information hierarchy, but the basic design made sense and there was an apparent need for this motivational feature to exist. Design

At this stage of the design project, it was time to focus on creating that positive, creative, supportive tone that feels inclusive and instills trust. Consumer Affairs reports that only 20% of consumers prefer a visit to a physical bank over a digital experience. Another statistic states that over half of Americans delay making financial decisions.What this says to me is there are a lot of individuals looking to have a positive experience with their personal finance tool, and they need a little guidance in making decisions to save and invest their money properly.

Conclusion

Being very clear on the design strategy helped the rest of this design fall into place easily later. I also think having an essential flow, or MVP, was a great strategy in the design process in order to prioritize the positive UX/UI experience. Throughout this process I learned to be more organized with documentation and handoff and more iterative in my design process. The next steps for this project would be to expand testing and functionality.
