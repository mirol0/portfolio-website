My Story

What if there were only two types of people in the world?

I’m talking about a simpler way to navigate life’s more puzzling challenges, like where to put your energy and what kind of career you’ll have. I subscribe to the idea that there are really, truly only two types of people, or rather, two eponymous personality types: Mozarts and Ben Franklins. Your Mozarts develop a passion early in life and devote their energy to perfecting their skill or craft. The others, the Benjamin Franklins, tend to nurture many interests and like to experiment with multiple life paths.

Now, as for me, I am a card carrying Ben Franklin. I am happiest when I am engaged in multiple interests, and I am comforted by the thought of a non-linear career path for the possibility of varied experiences. This way of experiencing the world has made me a dreamer, an idealist, a creative, and most recently: an adaptable, multi-skilled designer.

With over fifteen years in customer-facing roles in food service, education, and public health paired with becoming a mother and changing careers during a global pandemic, I like to think I know a thing or two about navigating life in flux! While I used to consider this lack of direction as a character flaw, I lovingly admire my Ben Franklin nature now as a part of me and a marketable skill. I know how to listen to my instinct, stretch outside my comfort zone, and be empathetic to a wide variety of people, which as a UX/UI designer who needs to understand the business goal and the user goal, is crucial to possess.

Currently based in Boulder, Colorado, I am seeking a full-time opportunity to leverage my skills and find mentorship and professional growth in a collaborative, inclusive environment with hybrid or work-from-home potential.

When I am not designing, I am often spotted playing with my son, baking, reading, making doodles or other crafts, walking my dog, and either skiing or looking forward to ski season.
